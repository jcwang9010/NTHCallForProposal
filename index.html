A solicitation to support the rapid transformation of emerging technologies into commercially viable, clinically-focused solutions to diagnose and treat disorders of the nervous system will soon be issued with funding provided by NIH.

The NIH Blueprint for Neuroscience Research is a collaborative effort supported by multiple NIH Institutes and Centers. Blueprint MedTech, one of its programs, aims to catalyze the translation of novel neurotechnologies from early-stage development to first-in-human clinical studies. Blueprint MedTech-funded incubator hubs (pending award) will provide: (a) non-dilutive funds to support medical device development activities led by investigators whose projects are selected for funding from the forthcoming call for proposals, and (b) additional resources and support services including, but not limited to:

  • Planning resources to support concept development, team building, needs assessment, and other early translational activities.
  • Streamlined access to translational services and expertise (e.g., design and prototyping, bench testing, large animal testing, biocompatibility assessment, manufacturing, medical monitoring).
  • Consultant services (e.g., on regulatory, reimbursement, intellectual property, commercialization, and strategic partnership issues).
  • Industry expertise (e.g., mentors and meetings with an external oversight committee).

The program seeks to increase the participation of applicants from underrepresented minorities

An official solicitation will be posted no later than September 27, 2022. We look forward to sharing more information soon.
